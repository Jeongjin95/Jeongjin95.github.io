Welcome!

I am a Ph.D. student in Biostatistics at The Ohio State University, advised by Dr. Patrick M. Schnell.

My research is centered on causal inference and infectious disease modeling, with a focus on developing statistical methods for complex biomedical and public health data. I am particularly interested in estimation and decision-making under censoring, missingness, and nonrandom observation processes.

Research Interests

Some of my research interests include:

  • Causal inference
  • Infectious disease modeling and surveillance
  • Survival analysis
  • Dynamic treatment regimes
  • Reinforcement learning for biomedical decision-making
  • Missing data and complex observation processes
