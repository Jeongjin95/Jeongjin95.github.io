Welcome!

I am a Ph.D. student in Biostatistics at The Ohio State University, advised by Dr. Patrick M. Schnell.

My primary research interests are in causal inference and infectious disease modeling. I am also interested in Q-learning and reinforcement learning for censored time-to-event outcomes, especially for individualized treatment decision-making under censoring, missingness, and complex observation processes.

For more details about my research, please click Research in the menu above.

Some of my research interests include:

  • Causal inference
  • Infectious disease modeling and surveillance
  • Q-learning and reinforcement learning for censored outcomes
  • Survival analysis
  • Dynamic treatment regimes
  • Missing data and complex observation processes

Research

My research focuses on developing statistical methods for causal inference, infectious disease modeling, survival analysis, and individualized decision-making in biomedical and public health studies.

Current Research Themes

  • Causal inference for infectious disease surveillance: developing counterfactual frameworks for prevalence estimation under nonrandom repeated testing, symptomatic testing, and contact tracing.
  • Infectious disease modeling: studying statistical methods for disease transmission, prevalence estimation, and surveillance data collected through complex observation processes.
  • Dynamic treatment regimes and reinforcement learning: developing Q-learning methods for censored time-to-event outcomes and individualized treatment decision-making.
  • Survival analysis and censored data: developing methods for high-dimensional, nonlinear, and incomplete survival outcomes.
